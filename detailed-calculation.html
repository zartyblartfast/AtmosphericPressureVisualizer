Understanding the Calculation Method

Our Atmospheric Pressure Calculator uses a well-established scientific method to estimate air pressure at different altitudes. This method combines the barometric formula and the International Standard Atmosphere (ISA) model. These models take into account various factors such as altitude, temperature, and the physical properties of air to provide an accurate estimation of atmospheric pressure.

Our Verification Process

We've taken steps to ensure the accuracy of our calculations. The calculation method has been thoroughly reviewed and cross-referenced with established scientific models and formulas. We've tested the calculator with a variety of inputs to ensure it works correctly and provides reliable results.

The Calculation Process in Detail

  1. Import constants for the temperature lapse rates for the 3 atmosphere layers included in our calculations (troposphere, stratosphere, and mesosphere).
  2. Define constants for gravitational acceleration (g), the molar mass of dry air (m), and the universal gas constant (R).
  3. Determine the temperature lapse rate based on the altitude. The lapse rate is the rate at which atmospheric temperature decreases with an increase in altitude. The lapse rate varies in different layers of the atmosphere.
  4. Calculate the temperature at the given altitude by subtracting the product of the lapse rate and the altitude from the standard temperature.
  5. Adjust the temperature from Celsius to Kelvin.
  6. Calculate the pressure at the given altitude using the formula for pressure in a gas at a certain height: pressure = seaLevelPressure * exp(-(g * m * altitude * 1000) / (R * temperatureAtAltitudeK)).
  7. Return the calculated pressure.
